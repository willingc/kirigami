# Current state

## discuss.python.org challenges

- hijacking a conversation
- derailing progress
- polarized views
- cadence
- volume of messages
- jumping into a discussion after a few days have passed
- difficult to share context and build common ground
- threading is fragile and breaks too often

## Discussion points

- Limit characters used in a response post
- How can we apply **Structured discussion tools**
- At scale, Discourse's thread-flattening problem isn't just annoying — it actively prevents decisions from being made.
- The dominant pattern: split synchronous from asynchronous, and separate discussion from decision

## Potential next steps

For 1000s of contributors and threads with 100s of messages, here's what I'd actually do, in order of leverage:

1. **Turn on Discourse AI summarization today** — lowest effort, immediate readability win. This is table stakes in 2026.
2. **Enable periodic summary reports** for high-traffic categories so people get weekly digests instead of trying to follow live.
3. **For roadmap threads specifically**, assign a human "thread steward" who posts a periodic structured summary as a wiki post — current positions, unresolved questions, next steps. LLM-assisted but human-reviewed. The act of someone owning the summary is often more valuable than the summary itself.
4. **For genuinely contested roadmap questions**, accept that summarization isn't enough and move to an RFC or Polis-style structured input process.

## Take-away

Summarization is a painkiller for the symptom; the disease is using a flat-thread medium for structured decision-making. Worth treating both.
